LSB Technique

Least Significant Bit (LSB) steganography modifies the last bit of each color channel in an image pixel. Since changing a single bit causes a color variation of only 1 out of 256 levels, the difference is imperceptible to human eyes.

  • Each pixel stores 3 bits (R, G, B channels)
  • 1 Megapixel image ≈ 375KB message capacity
  • PNG/BMP formats preserve bit-level data
  • JPEG compression destroys hidden data
