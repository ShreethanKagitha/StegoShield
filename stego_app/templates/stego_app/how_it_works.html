How It Works

The Magic of LSB Steganography

Steganography is the practice of concealing a file, message, image, or video within another file. Our platform uses the Least Significant Bit (LSB) algorithm to hide your sensitive data inside standard image files without noticeably changing their appearance.

1. The Encoding Process

Every digital image is made up of pixels. Each pixel has three color channels: Red, Green, and Blue (RGB), and each channel is represented by 8 bits (1 byte). The LSB algorithm takes the secret message, converts it into binary (0s and 1s), and replaces the last bit of each pixel's color channels with the bits of your secret message.

Because we only change the least significant bit, the color of the pixel changes by at most 1/256th of its value. This tiny shift is completely invisible to the human eye!

2. Encryption & Security

Before embedding the message, we optionally encrypt your text using military-grade AES-256 encryption. This means even if someone suspects there is a hidden message and manages to extract the bits, they will only see scrambled nonsense without your secure password.
